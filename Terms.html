Terms

  1. computer
    A programmable, electronic device that accepts data input, performs processing operations on that data, and outputs and stores the results.
  2. hardware
    The physical parts of a computer system, such as the keyboard, monitor, printer, and so forth.
  3. Internet
    The largest and most well-known computer network, linking millions of computers all over the world.
  4. processing
    Performing operations on data that has been input into a computer to convert that input to output.
  5. software
    The instructions, also called computer programs, that are used to tell a computer what it should do.
  6. storage
    The operation of saving data, programs, or output for future use.
  7. supercomputer
    The fastest, most expensive, and most powerful type of computer.
  8. tablet computer
    A portable computer about the size of a notebook that is designed to be used with a digital pen.
  9. Uniform Resource Locator (URL)
    An Internet address, usually beginning with http://, that uniquely identifies a Web page.
  10. Web site
    A collection of related Web pages usually belonging to an organization or individual.
  11. Data
    The raw, unorganized facts that are input into the computer to be processed.
  12. Application software (also called apps)
    Consists of programs designed to allow people to perform specific tasks or applications.
  13. Embedded computers
    Built into products (such as cars and household appliances) to give them added functionality.
  14. Mobile devices
    Small devices (such as mobile phones and media tablets) with computing or Internet capabilities.
  15. Smartphone
    An Internet-enabled mobile phone.
  16. Personal computers (PCs) or microcomputers
    Small computers used by individuals at home or work.
  17. Mainframe computers
    The powerful computers used by most large businesses and organizations to perform the information processing necessary for day-to-day operations are called mainframe computers.
  18. Internet service provider (ISP)
    A company that provides Internet access, usually for a fee.
  19. E-mail addresses
    A combination of a username and domain name that is used to send individual e-mail messages.
